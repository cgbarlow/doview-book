# How can we use DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams to get initiatives to do evidence-based practice?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](f6tool.md)

It is now widely accepted that evidence-based, or at least evidence-informed, policy and practice should be used. In many different sectors, the number of data repositories for evidence-based practice continues to increase. The number of individual evidence summaries they contain is also increasing at the same rate. However, as the movement spreads, the key question now is: 'What is the most efficient way of getting initiatives to adopt evidence-based practice?' The issue is how to motivate busy practitioners and planners to take the time to look at the evidence bases that are available when they are planning or reviewing their initiatives. Tailored AI summaries are being used to assist with this.

A complementary approach is to embed evidence within the tools that practitioners use in their day-to-day work. This has the advantage of providing evidence-based information in a way that immediately translates into the way they do their work. The more integrated such tools are into their routine planning and implementation work, the more likely practitioners are to use them. This happens if they are using an approach such as DoView planning.

This Embedding Evidence In DoView Strategy/Outcomes Diagram Templates Tool (F6) is focused on embedding evidence into template DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams. Practitioners and planners in the relevant field can pick up these template DoView diagrams and use them as a faster way to kick-start their planning. However, if the diagrams they are basing their planning on already have evidence embedded within them, just by working off these templates, practitioners will already be using an evidence-based approach. In fact, the practitioners and planners using such DoView diagram templates do not even need to be particularly aware that the diagrams they are using are evidence-based. The mere fact that they are using them is enough to get evidence-based practice occurring, regardless of practitioners' awareness that this is what is happening.
