# DoView Tool F4 — When Benchmarking is Appropriate Checklist

> **Pair:** [Question](f04question.md) · Tool (this page)

Benchmarking is the process of making comparisons between different organizations, providers, policies or initiatives. Conceptually it can be seen as comparing the whole of, or similar subpages of, an organization, agency, provider, policy or initiative's DoView strategy/outcomes diagram.

1. Do the initiatives to be compared have similar DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams or similar subpages within their DoView diagrams if their overall diagrams are different? IF YES, CONSIDER BENCHMARKING.

2. Compare the two subpages to assess whether both organizations have similar steps in their process, also compare with generic DoView subpages for the area they are focused on. TALK THROUGH WHY THERE ARE ANY STEPS MISSING IF THERE ARE.

2. Are the indicators that are being measured controllable by the initiative? IF YES, CONSIDER BENCHMARKING.

3. Have the different initiatives been running for a similar amount of time (where this is critical for them to be producing the results that are being compared). IF YES, CONSIDER BENCHMARKING.

4. Do the different initiatives that you are comparing have similar levels of resourcing? IF YES, CONSIDER BENCHMARKING.

5. Are the different initiatives being compared working with similar groups of people or units so that their results are comparable (see the discussion about comparing like-for-like in the When is League Tabling Appropriate Checklist (F5))? IF YES, THEN CONSIDER BENCHMARKING. IF NO, CAN 'IMPROVEMENT' BE COMPARED RATHER THAN JUST 'RAW' COMPARISONS.
