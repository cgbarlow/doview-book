# DoView Tool E10 — Public Private Partnership (PPP) Suitability Checklist

> **Pair:** [Question](e10question.md) · Tool (this page)

1. Is there clear evidence that private sector involvement will genuinely add value (e.g., through innovation or efficiency) that justifies higher financing costs compared to direct government borrowing? IF NOT, CONSIDER TRADITIONAL PUBLIC PROCUREMENT.

2. Are the risks being genuinely transferred to the private sector partner, or will government still be required to step in if the project encounters difficulties (e.g., financial distress, poor service delivery)? IF THE GOVERNMENT STILL CARRIES MOST OF THE RISK THE RATIONALE FOR PPP IS WEAKENED.

3. Has the government undertaken a thorough and transparent value-for-money assessment, including consideration of "whole-of-life" costs and public interest outcomes? IF NOT, DO SO.

4. Are the performance standards, service requirements, and contract terms clearly defined, measurable, and enforceable throughout the project lifecycle including using a DoView strategy/diagram and DoView Outcomes-Based Contracting? IF NOT, DO SO.

5. Is there sufficient public sector capability to manage and monitor the PPP contract, including the ability to enforce penalties and renegotiate terms if necessary? IF NOT, PUT IN PLACE CAPACITY OR DON'T DO PPP.

6. Are there safeguards in place to protect vulnerable users or service recipients and to ensure equitable access to services regardless of the PPP arrangement? IF NOT PUT THEM IN PLACE.

7. Have lessons from previous PPPs—locally and internationally—been reviewed and applied to mitigate common pitfalls? IF NOT, REVIEW AND LEARN FROM THEM.
