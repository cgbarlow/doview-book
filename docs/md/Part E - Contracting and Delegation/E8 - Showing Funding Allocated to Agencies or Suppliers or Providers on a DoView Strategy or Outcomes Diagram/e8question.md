# How can we better communicate the amount of money that has been allocated by a purchaser/funder for different purposes?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](e8tool.md)

Within any planning and funding system, there is a step that details the funding that is being provided to each supplier/provider. In the case of government funding, this is Step 8 in the Government Planning and Implementation Cycle [A1]. It also applies to other situations where purchasers/funders are funding multiple suppliers/providers/projects.

This information is typically set out in a table of some sort that shows the amount of money allocated to agencies/suppliers/providers to use for different purposes. Such tables tend to be dry and dense documents that are read by very few people, apart from those who prepare them and those who are required to read them as part of their work.

When using the DoView Planning approach, such tables can be brought to life by representing them against the same DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams that have been used in earlier strategy, planning, and prioritizations phases (e.g. Government Planning and Implementation Cycle (A2) (Steps 1 - 7).

Using this Showing Funding Allocated to Agencies/Suppliers/Providers on a DoView Strategy/Outcomes Diagram Explainer [E8], the amount of money allocated to agencies/suppliers/providers for different purposes is shown represented onto a DoView diagram.

In the case of each deliverables box, the dollar amount which has been allocated is shown in the small box in black print. You can then see the total amount that has been allocated to achieve the overall outcomes area. If you use an appropriate app or platform that can be used for DoView Planning, it will automatically total up the amount of funding in each outcome area\*.

It should be noted that to avoid siloization as discussed in the Not Siloing Steps Under Outcomes Explainer [B16], a project that has been allocated a budget should be able to apportion its budget across more than one outcome box if this reflects the fact that it is directed at influencing more than one outcome.

\* Such as the DoView legacy app.
