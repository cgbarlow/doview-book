# DoView Tool E12 — Social Bond Suitability Checklist

> **Pair:** [Question](e12question.md) · Tool (this page)

1. Is there clear evidence that the social bond approach will deliver genuine added value or improved outcomes? IF NOT CONSIDER WHETHER A STANDARD PUBLIC OR CONTRACTED MODEL WOULD BE MORE EFFECTIVE.

2. Can the provider cherry-pick clients who are more likely to improve than average clients in order to improve client outcomes? IF YES, CAN ANYTHING BE DONE TO PREVENT THIS? IF NOT, SOCIAL BONDS ARE NOT APPROPRIATE.

3. Can the provider queue-jump clients into supplementary services? Social services rely on professional networks, and access to supplementary services often depends on advocacy. Social bond clients may receive extra advocacy, creating an uneven playing field. IF YES, SOCIAL BONDS ARE NOT APPROPRIATE.

4. What is the cost to the government of the provider going out of business over the course of the social bond experiment? (See the When to Include an 'Assist' plus an 'Assess' Mode in Contracting Checklist (E7)).

5. Has a DoView strategy/outcomes diagram been used to spell out all aspects of the outcomes being sought by the social bonds scheme so that all parties are clear about responsibilities and risks? Is DoView Outcomes-Based Contracting being used in the scheme? IF NOT WHY NOT?

6. Has a thorough value-for-money assessment been conducted, considering whole-of-life costs and the impact on wider public services and equity? IF NOT WHY NOT.

7. Have lessons from previous social bond schemes—both local and international—been reviewed and used to inform risk mitigation and contract design? IF NOT WHY NOT.
