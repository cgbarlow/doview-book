# DoView Tool E5 — How an Agency, Provider, Policy, Organization or Initiative Can Prove it is Managing For Outcomes, Not Only Outputs

> **Pair:** [Question](e5question.md) · Tool (this page)

The initiative can submit to their control agency, purchaser/funder, auditors or other stakeholders what it is doing in each of the DoView Planning Framework (D1) areas as shown below.

## Diagram

| Phase | Component | What the initiative is doing |
|---|---|---|
| Strategy and planning | A — Evidence-based STRATEGY specified | DoView strategy diagram evidence-based and peer-reviewed |
| Strategy and planning | B — PRIORITIZATION of outcomes | Prioritization of outcomes clearly shown on our DoView diagram |
| Strategy and planning | C — ALIGNMENT of activities | Alignment shown by DoView Visual Alignment on our DoView |
| Monitoring performance & outcomes | D — OUTPUTS and indicators the initiative controls | Outputs and controllable indicators reported on in quarterly reports |
| Monitoring performance & outcomes | E — Higher-level OUTCOMES that are not-necessarily controllable | Trends in high-level outcomes tracked and reported on in quarterly reports (but attribution not established because not-necessarily controllable) |
| Evaluation | F — IMPLEMENTATION evaluation for optimization | DoView, initiative design discussed at conferences, expert peer review, feedback incorporated |
| Evaluation | G — PROCESS evaluation for description | All documentation archived, videos of staff and participants available |
| Evaluation | H — IMPACT evaluation to prove impact | Local University undertaking an impact evaluation |
| Evaluation | I — ECONOMIC and SUMMATIVE evaluation | Undertaken by funder showing hypothetical cost-benefits results |
