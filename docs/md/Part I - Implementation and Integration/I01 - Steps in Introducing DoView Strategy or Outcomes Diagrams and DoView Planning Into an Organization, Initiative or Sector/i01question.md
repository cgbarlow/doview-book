# What are the steps in progressively introducing the use of DoView Planning and DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams into planning, implementation, measurement and reporting?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](i01tool.md)

DoView Planning book includes 70+ tools, checklists, explainers, and examples for use in any organization/initiative. These fully integrated, theory-based (based on outcomes theory) tools have been tested in a wide range of settings.

As with any innovation, organizations and initiatives implement DoView Planning differently. New organizations can fully implement it from the start. Existing organizations may adopt selected elements initially, such as just using DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams for traffic lighting, priority setting and identifying indicators, especially if encouraged by funders.

Established organizations have existing planning, implementation, measurement, and impact assessment systems. The relationship between these and DoView Planning is shown in the Strategy and Outcomes Systems Terminology Tool (D2). Existing organizations can progressively introduce DoView Planning where it adds a value, complementing their current planning approaches.

Introducing DoView Planning into an established organization should be treated as a change management project. The tools are already developed, and the benefits of the visual DoView approach are obvious once an initiative starts using it. Still, widespread adoption requires some changes in attitudes, procedures, and documentation. Using DoView Planning and strategy/outcomes diagrams is a new discipline. Like any new discipline, it requires leadership to signal commitment to this approach.

Currently, DoView-type visual diagrams are often viewed as a helpful extra to verbal presentations and traditional documentation. DoView Planning seeks to move DoView diagrams to the center of planning, implementation, performance measurement, impact assessment, and reporting. In this approach, DoView diagrams are not just optional visuals—they become the central organizing artifact or "shared thinking tool" for strategy, planning, implementation, evaluation, and reporting.
