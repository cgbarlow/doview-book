# How should the workflow of high-level strategists, managers, politicians, and board members change to take advantage of strategy/outcomes diagrams and DoView Planning?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](i03tool.md)

As shown in the Steps in Introducing DoView Strategy/Outcomes Diagrams and DoView Planning Into an Organization, Initiative or Sector (I1), the DoView Planning approach aims to put the use of DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams at the heart of an organization or initiative's strategy, planning, implementation, performance measurement, impact assessment, and reporting. To achieve the efficiencies possible with DoView Planning, people at all levels within an organization or initiative need to adapt their working style.

Using DoView diagrams starts with people being able to use such diagrams on their own computers. In addition, in any strategy or outcomes meetings, the relevant DoViews should also be available as a shared thinking tool. Professional staff already interact with technical visual models of various kinds. However, real-time interaction with DoView diagrams in all meetings at all levels is different. A new convention needs to be introduced so all planning and implementation meetings have an interactive, editable DoView diagram of the issue being discussed. To facilitate this, meeting rooms should be set up using the How to Set up a Room for Interactive Strategy/Outcomes Diagram Work Checklist (I4).

Currently, higher-level managers, politicians, and board members generally spend less time with technical models than other staff. They are usually given summaries, presentations, and text-and-table documents, and take part in verbal discussions. With full DoView Planning implementation, high-level strategists, managers, politicians, and board members would adjust the way they work. They would spend more time interacting with DoView diagrams on their computers or with printed versions. In meetings, they would have data-projected DoViews or ones on their tablets or computers. This ensures that as discussions proceed, they can get a helicopter view of what is being discussed and immediately drill down to the relevant DoView subpage. As they become used to DoViews, high-level managers, politicians, and board members will realize how efficient DoViews are for understanding their organizations and initiatives, setting priorities, and ensuring alignment of action with priorities.
