# DoView Tool I3 — Changing the Work Flow of High-Level Strategists, Managers, Politicians and Board Members To Facilitate Them Using DoView Planning Explainer

> **Pair:** [Question](i03question.md) · Tool (this page)

For DoView Planning to be fully effective, high-level managers, politicians and board members, need to make a few changes to the way they currently work.

## Diagram

### High-level managers, politicians and board members current approach

| # | Current activity |
|---|---|
| 1 | Working out in their minds the outcomes they want to achieve and the high-level timeframes, resources, and trade-offs needed. |
| 2 | Reading text-and-table strategy, planning and reporting documents from agencies, and trying to work out if they are fully aligned with outcomes. |
| 3 | Listening to verbal reports and presentations and trying to work out if they are fully aligned with outcomes. |
| 4 | Discussing with management and staff aspects of strategy, planning, and reporting and trying to work out if they are fully aligned with outcomes. |
| 5 | Making decisions about strategy and resource allocation and attempting to identify gaps and overlaps in investment. |

### Changes needed to use DoView diagrams and DoView Planning

| # | New activity |
|---|---|
| 1 | Being involved in developing DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams that fully capture the organization or initiative's outcomes and the steps that it is believed are necessary to achieve them. |
| 2 | Interacting with electronic versions of DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams to see that they include the outcomes being sought, the steps that are needed, current priorities, relevant indicators and DoView Visual Alignment between priorities and projects or activities has been achieved. |
| 3 | Listening to management and staff presentations based around working in real-time with projected, or mobile device, interactive DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams helping them understand where what is being presented fits within the overall strategic directions and outcomes. |
| 4 | When talking to management or staff about any aspect of strategy, planning, implementation and measurement against the relevant DoView diagram so that at any moment they can drill-down to assure themselves that what is being done is fully aligned with outcomes. |
| 5 | Using the relevant DoView strategy/outcomes diagram when making resource allocation and budgeting decisions and employing DoView Visual Alignment between priority boxes and projects or activities to identify gaps and overlaps in current investment. |
