# How should you set up a meeting room for serious interactive DoView strategy/outcomes diagram work?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](i04tool.md)

Different professions have customized work environments. Lawyers work in specially designed courtrooms, surgeons in highly technical operating theatres, financial traders use desks with large numbers of screens. Their work and the technology they use determine how workspaces are set up.

At its core, DoView Planning is about making interactive DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams central to all decision-making. To do this, we need to optimize the physical and virtual environments in which politicians, boards, managers, and professionals work. When people work at desks, with the right apps or platforms, they can have a rich interaction with the electronic version of an organization or initiative's DoView diagram. To fully achieve DoView Planning benefits, the possibility of rich interactivity must be carried over to the other place where people do much of their work, the real or online meeting room.

When using a DoView diagram, anyone in a meeting needs to be able to see, read, and the interaction with the diagram. This can be achieved in two ways: either optimizing the set-up of the room's digital displays or projector screens, or making sure everyone in the room screen shares the DoView on their tablet or computer. As the meeting proceeds they can then see the DoView as it is discussed and edited in real time. The size the standard drill-down subpages in a DoView has been designed so that they are of a size that can be data-projected on a standard-sized screen or viewed on a tablet or computer. It is essential that people using a DoView feel that they are in a position to fully understand it. In addition, the group needs to feel that it is free to change aspects of the DoView at any time to make it better reflect what the organization or initiative is trying to do.

Furthermore, there should be plenty of wall or window space in meeting rooms for poster versions of DoViews to be put up either just for the duration of a meeting or for final versions of DoViews to be put up permanently. When you do this you often find people standing in front of the posters discussing strategy by referring to the DoView because they can see the strategy spelled out in full on it. If an organization wants people to quickly understand what it is doing they can put a well presented poster version of their DoView up in their office foyer and on their website so people can immediately get an overview of what they are doing.
