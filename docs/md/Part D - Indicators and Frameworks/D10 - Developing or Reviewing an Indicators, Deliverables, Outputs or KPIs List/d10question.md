# How can you develop or review a list of deliverables or indicators (e.g. outputs or KPI) list?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](d10tool.md)

The features of the indicators (measures) that should be included in a deliverables list (indicators, outputs or KPIs list) are covered in the Deliverables Outputs and KPIs Suitability Definition Checklist (D9). If you are doing Types 1- 3 from the Types of Contracting for Outcomes or Outputs (E3) limit your deliverables list to only include controllable indicators. Just measuring these happened proves that the initiative made them happen - they are therefore 'attributable' to it. If doing outcomes-based contracting for higher-level not-necessarily controllable outcomes, be transparent and formally set up Type 4 - Contracting for Outcomes from the Types of Contracting for Outcomes or Outputs Tool (E3).

The best way to develop a deliverables list is to do it from the appropriate DoView strategy/outcomes diagram for the organization, agency, provider, policy or initiative. Look at the DoView onto which indicators have been placed using the Placing Indicators Onto a DoView Strategy/Outcomes Diagram Tool (D5). Then select your deliverables from the boxes that are controllable by the initiative and that have indicators conforming to the Deliverables, Outputs and KPIs Suitability Checklist (D9). In the convention often used in DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams, these are indicators that have an '@' next to them. Typically people select boxes at the highest level of controllability within the DoView diagram or outcomes framework rather than ones lower down the diagram. These are usually measures of the quality, quantity or timeliness of goods and services produced by the organization, agency, provider, policy or initiative (the typical definition of an output is that it is a good or a service produced by an initiative).

You can use the same approach when you review a deliverables list (indicator, outputs or KPI list). When reviewing it, build the implicit strategy/outcomes diagram underlying the deliverables list whether they are currently measurable or not and populate it with the indicators that are included in the deliverables list being reviewed. Look at this strategy/outcomes diagram to see if there are any additional boxes with controllable indicators that could be included within the deliverables list. If you are using Type 1 - 3 contracting from the Types of Contracting for Outcomes or Outputs (E3), you just need to check that the indicators in the deliverables lists conform to the requirements of the Deliverables, Outputs and KPIs Suitability Checklist (D7).
