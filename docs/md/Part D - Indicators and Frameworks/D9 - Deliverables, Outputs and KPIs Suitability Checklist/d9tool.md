# DoView Tool D9 — Deliverables, Outputs and KPIs Suitability Checklist

> **Pair:** [Question](d9question.md) · Tool (this page)

Deliverables lists under types 1 - 3 of the Four Types of Contracting For Outcomes Or Outputs (E3) should only include elements that are the following:

1. RELEVANT - to achieving higher-level outcomes within the DoView strategy diagram or outcomes framework for the agency, provider, policy or initiative.

2. MEASURABLE - able to be measured.

3. CONTROLLABLE - under the control of the initiative, (e.g. goods or services it produces (outputs)), not significantly influenced by other factors. This means that merely measuring them will prove that it is the initiative that made them happen. They are therefore ATTRIBUTABLE to the initiative. This does not prevent higher level not-necessarily controllable indicators being included when contracting for 'managing for outcomes' rather than contracting for 'accountability for outcomes'.\*

4. AFFORDABLE - you can afford to measure it.

5. NON-MANIPULABLE - cannot be gamed by the initiative to pretend that it has delivered it when it has not.

6. COMPREHENSIVELY MEASURED - ideally, each deliverable will have indicators for its QUALITY, QUANTITY and TIMELINESS.

7. HIGH ENOUGH UP THE DOVIEW STRATEGY DIAGRAM OR OUTCOMES FRAMEWORK - usually, many boxes within a DoView strategy/outcomes diagram or outcomes framework have the above features. The lower down the diagram, the more of them you will find. Therefore, you usually limit deliverables lists to only those boxes at the 'top edge' of where controllability finishes - these are often described as outputs ('goods or services' the initiative produces). However, as discussed in the Search for Non-Output Controllable Indicators Explainer (D12), there are sometimes controllable indicators above the level of goods or services you can delegate or contract for. This is why DoView Planning provides for 'contracting for controllables' in addition to 'contracting for outputs'.\*

\* See the Four Types of Contracting For Outcomes or Outputs (E3) for a more detailed discussion.
