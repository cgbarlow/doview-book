# DoView Tool A5 — Questions Politicians, Boards, CEOs, High-Level Management and Monitoring Agencies Need Answered Explainer

> **Pair:** [Question](a5question.md) · Tool (this page)

Politicians responsible for organizations and initiatives, boards, CEOs, top management, and in the case of government, monitoring agencies, need to have a quick way of effectively answering the key questions listed below. However, there is currently no standard, widely applied framework for communicating this strategic information. In contrast, in the area of accounting, there are detailed accounting standards that specify how organizational financial information needs to be spelled out which means the financial health of any organization can be rapidly assessed. In the same way, there could be a standardized and widely applied way of structuring the strategy, planning, implementation and reporting information needed to answer the questions below. If this existed and was widely used, those charged with oversight of organizations and initiatives would be able to quickly assess how any organization which is using the approach is doing regarding its strategy and achieving its outcomes. The DoView Planning approach, including the use of DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams provides such a standardized methodology for doing this with any type of organization or initiative. The approach can also be used in a situation where a number of organizations and stakeholders are coordinating what they are doing in a sector.

1. What is the sector's, organization's or initiative's current strategic direction and priorities, and how can these be quickly changed if inappropriate?

2. Is the sector, organization or initiative being distracted from its strategic mission for any reason and neglecting any important areas of its work and priorities?

3. Are its indicators and KPIs measuring the most important aspects of its work?

4. Can it prove that there are no gaps or overlaps in where it is investing in its activities or projects and they are tightly aligned to its priorities?

5. What evaluation activity is taking place to provide the type of information needed for future strategic decision-making?
