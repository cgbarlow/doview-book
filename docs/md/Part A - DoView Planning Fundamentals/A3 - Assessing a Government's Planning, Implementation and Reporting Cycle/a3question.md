# How can we use the government planning, implementation and reporting cycle to check a particular government's planning process?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](a3tool.md)

If we want to check and potentially improve how a government is doing its planning, implementation and reporting, we can use the Assessing a Government's Planning, Implementation and Reporting Cycle Tool (A2) in the following way.

First, familiarize yourself with how the particular government's process works. It is often not easy to do this quickly because how governments undertake their planning, action, and reporting cycle depends on the particular government's history and institutions. However AI could be of assistance here to review the documentation.

Obviously there is the need to read some of the documentation and guidance regarding the particular government's functioning. But being practical, the most effective way of reviewing a specific government's system is to call together a group of key officials who fully understand how their particular government's system works.

You can then take use this Assessing a Government's Planning, Implementation and Reporting Cycle Assessment Tool (A3). Take them through each step in the cycle and have them explain how their system currently operates and to critically examine how well it does the particular step and review what you have found out using an AI analysis.

At the end of this process, you should have a clear idea of whether the particular government system contains all of the steps in the cycle. You will also have collected some information about whether this government is implementing the particular step in the process well.

Deciding how well a government is doing a step in the cycle requires an understanding of current international best practice in regard to that particular step. You may have this knowledge yourself or you may need to involve AI or one or more experts to help assess the particular step.
