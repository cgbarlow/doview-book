# How can DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams be used at each point in the Government Planning, Implementation and Reporting Cycle?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](a4tool.md)

DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams can be used in many steps in the Government Planning Implementation and Reporting Cycle (A2). They increase the speed, efficiency and transparency of implementing those steps. This Using DoView Strategy Diagrams in the Government Planning, Implementation and Reporting Cycle Tool (A4) shows where they can be used in different steps in the cycle.

At the start of the cycle, DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams can be drawn for politicians' outcomes, citizens' outcomes, and government agency business-as-usual outcomes. These may be separate diagrams, OR THEY MAY BE INTEGRATED INTO A SINGLE DOVIEW. Looking at the diagram(s), the various parties can see their concerns have been incorporated. DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams can also be drawn for cross-agency initiatives if wished.

The same DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams that are drawn at the start of the cycle (or amended from previous planning cycles) can then be used throughout the cycle as is shown in this tool. They can form the basis for co-design, collective impact and other discussions between politicians, stakeholders, government agencies, government control agencies, service providers, and the public in the course of other steps within the cycle, making the whole process more transparent. It also helps identify the sometimes unintended consequences arising from making trade-offs between stakeholder groups.

Using DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams throughout the Government Planning Implementation and Reporting Cycle (A2) makes it easier for politicians, government agencies, stakeholders and the public to check that the outcomes, steps and priorities emerging from discussions in the cycle have been fully captured. Politicians and government agencies can also be assured of tight alignment between agreed priorities and the actions being taken. This means that there is likely to be less slippage than with traditional ways of doing strategy and outcomes work. The traditional approach is to use text and table documents and verbal planning discussions. However, these do not have the communicative and analytical power of the DoView Planning approach.
