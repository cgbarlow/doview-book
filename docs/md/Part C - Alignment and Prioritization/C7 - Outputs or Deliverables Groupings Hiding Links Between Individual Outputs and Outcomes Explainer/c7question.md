# How can grouping outputs/deliverables obscure the link between outputs and higher-level outcomes?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](c7tool.md)

In some strategy, planning, implementation, and reporting systems, 'outputs' ('deliverables' or 'activities') are gathered into larger groups (e.g. 'outputs groups' or 'outputs classes'). This makes it simpler for decision-makers to specify and control funding because they can just allocate funding to such larger groups of outputs rather than individual outputs. This is useful, but fine-grained information can be lost if the links between individual outputs and outcomes are only reported at a group level. To understand this, it is easiest to think in terms of a DoView strategy/outcomes diagram.

There is no problem where all of the individual output boxes within an output group happen to just be focused on a single higher-level step or outcome. In such a case, information about exactly which outcomes each individual output box links to is not obscured because all of the outputs just link to the single higher-level outcomes box.

But as we know, to properly portray reality, we need to allow any output box to potentially link to any higher-level step or outcome box within a DoView strategy/outcomes diagram. See the Do Not Silo Steps Under Outcomes Explainer (B16). Situations often arise where a lower-level individual output box links to more than one higher-level step or outcome box. If this happens a great deal, then a visual representation that only shows the links between output group heading and high-level steps or outcomes is not sufficiently detailed. Most outcomes group headings will link to most higher-level outcomes boxes within such a representation, but the reader will not be able to work out the specific link between individual outputs and higher-level steps or outcomes.

Within DoView Planning, the solution to this is to provide the whole DoView strategy/outcomes diagram in addition to whatever summaries you may have, just showing how output group headings link to higher-level steps or outcomes. With a fully articulated DoView diagram, the reader can look at the entire structure and see the linkages that exist from each lower-level output box to each higher-level step or outcome box. They can then determine from such a fine-grained visualization, whether there is, or is not, sufficient 'line-of-sight' alignment between outputs and outcomes.
