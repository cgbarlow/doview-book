# Is the Balanced Scorecard approach compatible with the DoView Planning approach?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](h3tool.md)

The Balanced Scorecard (BSC) is one of several indicator frameworks used in the private sector, and its use has also migrated to the public sector. It consists of four headings for grouping indicators (below) and the concept of a 'strategy map'.

Financial
Customer
Learning and growth
Internal business processes

To use the Balanced Scorecard approach within DoView Planning, you just need to take the indicators identified from a DoView strategy/outcomes diagram and group them under the Balanced Scorecard headings. In this Deriving a Balanced Scorecard Format From a Strategy/Outcomes Diagram Explainer (H3) you can see that the indicators from the Archery Initiative DoView strategy/outcomes diagram have been simply reorganized under the four BSC headings. In a suitable app *, you can save time by simply 'cloning' these indicators from where they appear on the DoView diagram and put them as clones on a separate page grouped under the four Balanced Scorecard headings. Then, wherever you update the indicators in the DoView file, they will change everywhere their clones appear.

The other aspect of the Balanced Scorecard approach is the use of 'strategy maps'. These are visual models that set out in boxes the different indicators from the balanced scorecard under the four headings. Strategy maps pioneered the use of visual models in planning and have some similarities to DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams used in DoView Planning. However, they are usually set out with the boxes grouped under the four BSC headings, which is not the best way of showing 'This-Then' flow. They are also usually not broken up into drill-down subpages that are meaningful to readers in the same way that DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams are. In addition, they are usually limited to only currently quantifiable boxes. A key point about DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams is that they can contain currently unquantified boxes because strategy discussions should not be limited to just the currently measurable.

\* For instance DoView Strategy and Outcomes Software
