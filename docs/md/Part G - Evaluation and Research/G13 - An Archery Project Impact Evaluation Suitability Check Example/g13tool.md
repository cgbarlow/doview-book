# DoView Tool G13 — An Archery Project Impact Evaluation Suitability Check Example

> **Pair:** [Question](g13question.md) · Tool (this page)

This is a worked example of an Impact Evaluation Suitability Check for the illustrative Archery Project (B4). For our purposes here the Archery Project is defined as the training and equipping of a group of archers.

## Diagram

| # | Design type | Decision | Appropriate | Feasible | Affordable | Credible |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | True experimental designs | NO | YES. Could be an appropriate design. | NO. There is only one archery range, there is no way to stop the members of the control group watching the training and going out and buying the same equipment so this design is unlikely to show the project improved outcomes. | NO. Would have to employ specialist outcome evaluators and have to pay control group archerys also, it is beyond the budget allocated for evaluation. | YES. Would have been credible with stakeholders if able to be done. |
| 2 | Time Series designs | YES | YES. Does not require a control group; the intervention can start at a specific point in time. | YES. The intervention starts at a specific point in time, the target automatically records arrows hitting it. There is a sufficient number of arrows hitting the target over time to do the analysis. | YES. Project staff can do the analysis. | YES. Will be credible to many stakeholders. |
| 3 | Constructed comparison group designs | NO | YES. If could find a suitable comparison groups. | NO. There is no other archery range which has a target that automatically records the number of arrows that hit it. | NO. Could not afford to pay for observer to record every hit of an arrow on the target. | YES. Would have been credible if an archery club that was very similar to the intervention group could have been found. |
| 4 | Alternative explanations elimination designs | (not marked) | YES. It is automatically recorded when arrows hit the target. | YES. We will be able to develop a list of alternative explanations and analyze them as to whether they are causing the arrows to hit the target rather than the project. | YES. A project staff member can write the report. | MODERATE. |
| 5 | Expert judgment designs | YES | YES. A group of archery experts are in a position to comment on the success of the project. | YES. We have identified the experts. | YES. Will ask funder for a small amount of funding to cover this as they are visiting the archery club anyway. | MODERATE. Should be reasonably credible to archery stakeholders. |
| 6 | Key Informant judgment designs | NO | NO. There is no group of independent key informants that could comment on whether the project was effective or not. No one apart from archers are allowed on the archery range. | NO. Not feasible as no one else on the archery range. | NO. Would normally involve a number of key informant interviews that would cost too much to undertake. | NO. Not credible in this case as there are no independent observers to make the judgment of effectiveness. |
| 7 | Intervention logic Model (theory-based) designs | YES | YES. Have developed the project's DoView strategy/outcomes diagram and had it peer reviewed. | YES. The project staff can do the analysis. | YES. The project staff can do the work. | LOWER. Will be regarded as less credible by some stakeholders. To discuss with funder. |
