# What is a worked example of doing an impact evaluation suitability check for an initiative?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](g13tool.md)

The purpose of the Archery Project Impact Evaluation Suitability Check Example (G11) is to show, step-by-step, how the Impact Evaluation Suitability Check Tool's (G10) four criteria—appropriateness, feasibility, affordability, and credibility—can be applied to a concrete initiative: the Archery Project, which equips and trains a group of archers. Like G10, the example goes through the full range of impact-evaluation design types, assessing each one against the four criteria.

The idea is for an analysis such as this, which has been done for a specific project, to form the basis of discussion between a supplier/provider and their purchaser/funder or control agency regarding what impact evaluation should be undertaken. This enables a coherent, comprehensive and robust discussion to take place concerning which impact evaluation designs (if any) the funder wants undertaken. It also documents that the project has undertaken due diligence into the possibilities for impact evaluation designs.

AI can help with this by providing a first draft analysis of the appropriateness, feasibility, affordability and credibility of the different impact evaluation designs. This can then be checked and further developed by the project to provide the final version of the suitability check they will take to their purchaser/funder or control agency. While a supplier/provider doing this analysis has been discussed here, as pointed out in the Types of Reporting and Accountability a Control Agency or Funder Can Require From an Initiative (E1), a purchaser/funder may do workup this suitability check, or get evaluation experts to do so.
