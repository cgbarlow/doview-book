# DoView Tool G19 — Relationship Between Discounting and the Ease of Intervening Explainer

> **Pair:** [Question](g19question.md) · Tool (this page)

Discounting in cost-benefit analysis is used to take into account the fact that if you wait to intervene you can get interest by investing later. However cost-benefit analyses also need to take into account whether the ease of intervening over time increases. If it does this also needs to be taken into account in the analysis.

## Diagram

| | Case 1 — Intervention costs stay the same | Case 2 — Intervention costs rise over time |
|---|---|---|
| **Acting now** | Fixes the problem | Fixes the problem |
| **Acting later** | Fixes the problem, costs the same, but you have earned interest by waiting (financial gain) | Costs much more to fix the problem, or it might become unfixable. Any interest gained from waiting may be outweighed by higher cost and risk. |

**Economic analysis should weigh the benefits of earning interest against the higher cost and risk from delaying.**
