# DoView Tool G23 — The Social Problem Strategies Continuum

> **Pair:** [Question](g23question.md) · Tool (this page)

There is a range of different strategies for addressing social problems as set out below. For each of them there is a continuum running from the easiest actions that can be taken through to actions that are more challenging to implement.

## Diagram

Strategies sit within **Development and implementation of evidence-based solutions**, leading to **problem solution or amelioration**. Within each strategy, the continuum runs from easiest (top — less resistance) to most challenging (bottom — more effective).

| Educational / Informational | Community Mobilization | Monitoring and Regulatory Enforcement | Institutional Development | Policy, Rules and Standards Development |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Knowledge acquisition | Community support | Raising awareness of regulatory requirements | Networking | Identifying policy options |
| Attitudinal change | Voicing community concerns | Increasing regulatory system responsiveness | Coordination | Providing information about policy options |
| Behavioral change | Co-production | Proactive enforcement | Restructuring / building new institutional infrastructure | Generating debate on policy options |
| Increasing awareness of the situation and rights | Real change on the ground | | | Coalitions to support new positive policy options |

Encouragement from supportive stakeholders pushes movement *down* the continuum (towards more effective action). Resistance from currently non-supportive stakeholders pushes movement *up* the continuum (towards lower-resistance, less effective action).
