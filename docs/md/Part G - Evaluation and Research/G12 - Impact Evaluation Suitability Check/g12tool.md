# DoView Tool G12 — Impact Evaluation Suitability Check

> **Pair:** [Question](g12question.md) · Tool (this page)

Use this tool to work out whether any particular type of impact evaluation is appropriate, feasible, affordable, or credible for a particular initiative. Use it to help identify the best type of impact evaluation to use, if any. Also, use it to prove 'due diligence' to purchasers/funders or control agencies of the possible types of impact evaluation have been considered by an initiative.

## Diagram

| # | Design type | Appropriate | Feasible | Affordable | Credible |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | True Experimental Designs | Need to be able to create a credible control intervention (placebo) different from the intervention; must be ethical to withhold intervention from controls. | Need to be able to stop controls exposure to the intervention. | Usually expensive due to the level of administration and control needed. | Regarded as highly credible by many key stakeholders because it controls for external factors affecting outcomes. |
| 2 | Time Series designs | Does not require a control group; the intervention needs to start at a specific point in time. | Needs sufficient number of observations over time to do the analysis. | Often affordable if the data is already being collected. | Is credible to many stakeholders. |
| 3 | Constructed Comparison Group Designs | Overcomes ethical problem of deliberately withholding intervention from a control group. | You do not need to do randomized allocation to control and intervention. | Usually less expensive than true experimental design. | Less credible as may be other factors determining why intervention and comparison group differ rather than just the intervention. |
| 4 | Alternative Explanation Elimination Designs | Where there are problems with other impact designs. | Easier to implement than true experimental designs. | Usually more affordable. | Less credible to many stakeholders. |
| 5 | Expert Judgment Designs | Where the field includes recognized experts. | Need a credible independent expert. | Usually more affordable if funding can be obtained for an outside expert. | As credible as the expert is regarded as. |
| 6 | Key Informant Judgment Designs | Where a group of people can be aware of the working of an initiative; who are likely to provide an unbiased assessment. | Need to be able to get key informants to participate. | Usually more affordable. | As credible as the group of key informants. |
| 7 | Intervention logic Model (Theory-Based) Designs | Where other designs are not possible. | Where there is a clear evidence-informed strategy/outcomes diagram. | More affordable as can be done at desk. | Regarded as less credible as is based on making the assumption that lower level boxes have caused improvements in higher-level ones. |

As you move down from row 1 to row 7, the **Appropriate**, **Feasible**, and **Affordable** ratings generally increase, while the **Credible** rating to critical stakeholders generally (but not always) reduces.
