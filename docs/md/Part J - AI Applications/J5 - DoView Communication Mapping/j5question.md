# How could DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams potentially be used to structure all your email, text and chat communication?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](j5tool.md)

DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams are designed to capture the 'This-Then' logic underlying any attempt at taking action in the world. As shown in DoView Planning, they can serve as the foundation for all aspects of planning, implementation, measurement, and management.

In any business environment, and much of our personal lives, we are constantly trying to take action. To do this, we spend a considerable amount of time communicating with others. However, there are challenges in how we currently communicate when we need to get things done. First, we use multiple channels: emails, texts, WhatsApp, Messenger, Signal, Telegram, Slack, LinkedIn, and so on. We need to check each of these different apps for messages. Second, the messages themselves are often a mix, some focus on a single piece of information, while others, such as emails, may contain several topics. Third, to determine our next actions, we must map these scattered pieces of information onto our internal mental model of the steps needed to achieve our outcomes. This process involves a significant cognitive load.

From a DoView Planning perspective, the key is always to externalize our mental 'This-Then' models of action by turning them into DoView diagrams. Once we have drawn such a DoView diagram (or, better still, have AI draw it, see our free AI DoView Drawing Prompt at DoViewPlanning.Org), this opens up the interesting possibility of using it to collate and organise all of our currently disparate communications. The Using DoView Prompts as a Complement to Everyday Language Prompts for AI Agents (J4) Tool already points in this direction, proposing that we get AI to create a DoView of what it thinks we want it to achieve, and then AI using this DoView to report progress and request further information. In the same way, the Mapping All Our Communications Onto an AI-Generated DoView Tool (J5) proposes extending this way of working to all of our incoming and outgoing communications.
