# DoView Tool J1 — Areas Where Outcomes Theory and DoView Planning May Apply to AI Agents Explainer

> **Pair:** [Question](j01question.md) · Tool (this page)

As AI agents begin to be directed to act in the world, they face similar issues to those that humans do, which are addressed in outcomes theory and DoView Planning - planning, implementing, measuring and managing what they do. Here are some examples that could be explored of how DoView Planning might be used by AI agents in their work.

The high-level action that AI is planning to take when it approaches any assignment could be specified by it drawing a DoView strategy/outcomes diagram of the outcomes it is pursuing and the steps it intends to take to get to them (B4 and B7).

AI agents could potentially be required to show in more detail how they are planning to undertake the individual boxes in the DoView diagram they have produced using DoView Visual Alignment (C3).

In AI orchestration, where several AI agents are contributing to the same goal, there are issues related to the trade-off between attribution, accountability, and encouraging collaboration.

When humans get AI agents to undertake work, within DoView Planning, this is can be seen as them delegating the AI agent to work on the whole of, or part of the relevant DoView strategy/outcomes diagram. When doing this one should think in terms of the DoView Planning Framework (D1) and the Delegating Resourcing Trade-Offs When Amalgamating Agencies Risk Checklist (E9).

The different types of DoView diagrams - Planning, Evidence-Based, Implementation and Evaluated DoViews - may be relevant to AI systems.

In terms of impact evaluation of AI interventions, the Impact Evaluation Suitability Check (G11) could be used.

AI agents could use the Good Indicator Checklist (D6) to attach behavioral, technical and real-world metrics to boxes within the relevant DoView diagram. As AI is achieving boxes it could be reporting back by traffic lighting the boxes in relevant parts of the DoView diagram.
