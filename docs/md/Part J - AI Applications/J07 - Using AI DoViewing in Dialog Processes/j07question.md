# How can AI DoViewing assist dialog processes where groups discuss differing perspectives?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](j07tool.md)

DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams capture the 'This-Then' logic of any situation in which we want to take action. Naturally, different groups may have differing views on what should be represented in a DoView. Differences may arise regarding the outcomes being sought on the right-hand side of the DoView, the steps believed necessary to achieve these outcomes, or even the sequence of steps within the diagram.

Various dialog processes have been developed to enable groups with different perspectives to share their views in a safe environment. One such methodology is the Rich Dialog Process (RDP). This brings together two or more groups with potentially differing views to help them better understand each other's perspectives. The goal is not to force consensus but to encourage deeper thinking about viewpoints and possible alternative perspectives others may hold. Rich Dialog Processes can be set up in several ways and may involve multiple groups. When two groups are involved, each first meets separately with an independent facilitator to discuss the issue at hand. They then draft a statement to send to the other group, and each group puts questions to the other about their views. Following this, both groups come together in a facilitated session, not to force consensus on the topic but to help participants gain different perspectives.

The Rich Dialog Process may potentially be enhanced through the use of DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams. Each group would meet and, with the assistance of their facilitator and AI*, develop a DoView diagram reflecting their views on desired outcomes, the steps required to achieve them (including risks and opportunities), and priority actions. Then when the groups get to meet together, similarities and differences in their DoViews could be explored in a facilitated discussion. AI could also help in comparing the DoViews' similarities and differences. This tool should be seen as just one exploration of ways in which AI's ability to quickly and independently develop DoView diagrams could be used to facilitate more fruitful discussions between groups about the outcomes they are seeking and ways of achieving them. The same approach could potentially be applied to other types of dialog and related processes.

\* If you are interested in having AI draw DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams see DoViewPlanning.Org.
