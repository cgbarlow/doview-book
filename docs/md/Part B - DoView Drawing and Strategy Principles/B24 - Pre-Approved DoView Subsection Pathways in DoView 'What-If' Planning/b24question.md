# Can you pre-approve your pathway through particular What-If DoView subsections in order to speed up implementation when under time pressure?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](b24tool.md)

DoView What-If Planning (B23) shows how you can do scenario planning as part of DoView Planning. When doing DoView What-If Planning, you can use another tool, DoView Subsection Pre-Approval. In some cases, you may have large DoViews with many subsections. For much of the DoView, you proceed as in standard DoView Planning, discussing and then selecting particular boxes as priorities based on the current strategic landscape and the progress you are making on other boxes in your DoView. Then, if the threshold of one or more trigger indicators is crossed, you move to a new Current What-If DoView subsection.

In some cases, you are happy to proceed as normal and discuss and prioritize amongst the boxes within the new Current What-If DoView subsection. When doing this, you have to, of course, take time to determine your strategic options as to which boxes you will prioritize.

However, in some situations, you know in advance that you will need to act quickly if particular What-If DoView subsections are triggered. In such cases, you may decide that it is better to pre-approve implementing a specific pathway within a What-If DoView subsection. Doing this means that as soon as the What-If subsection has been triggered, you can act immediately because decision-makers have already pre-approved the specific course of action. When under time pressure, this means you can move through your overall DoView faster.
