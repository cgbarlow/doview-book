# What does a real-world interactive drill-down DoView strategy/outcomes diagram look like?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](b06tool.md)

This tool shows an example of an interactive drill-down DoView strategy/outcomes diagram drawn using the DoView Drawing Rules (B7). This particular strategy diagram is concerned with regional development. The top overview page in the DoView shows a series of boxes that are the steps that it is believed need to occur to achieve the high-level regional outcome of 'A sustainable, economically thriving inclusive community'. This overview page is designed to give a 'helicopter view' of the strategy.

Each of the boxes on the overview page can be clicked on to reveal the contents of its drill-down subpage. These drill-down subpages give more detail of the 'This-Then' logic of the steps it is believed need to occur to make progress in subpage. In this example, the drill-down subpage page for 'Economic Exuberance' is shown.

Such DoView subpages can be printed out and used as stand-alone images in strategy discussions. They can be made up into a poster page showing the overview and all of the subpages all at once. They can also be included as images in strategy, planning and reporting documents as outlined in the How to Put a DoView Strategy Diagram into Existing Government Planning and Reporting Documents Tip (I2).

In addition, if you used the How to Set up a Room For Interactive DoView Strategy Diagram Work Checklist (I4) and appropriate software* the strategy diagram can be used interactively in real-time during meetings.

When used in this way, meeting participants can look at the top overview page to disucss the broad outlines of the strategy. At any moment, you can drill-down to a lower-level subpage and use it to elaborate on what needs to happen at that level. When participants have finished, you can immediately move back up to the overview page and, whenever necessary, you can then simply drill-down to another subpage.

Using a DoView strategy diagram in this interactive way is much faster than having to refer meeting participants to particular pages within a text-based planning document.

\* For instance PowerPoint or Google Slides or the DoView Planning legacy app.
