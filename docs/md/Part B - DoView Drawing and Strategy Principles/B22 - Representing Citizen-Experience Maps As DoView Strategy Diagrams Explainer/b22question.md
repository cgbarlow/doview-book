# What should be used to articulate citizens' outcomes (citizen-experience DoView strategy diagrams)?

> **Pair:** Question (this page) · [Tool](b22tool.md)

As is shown in the Politicians, Government Agency Business-As-Usual, and Citizen Outcomes Integration Tool (B20), specifying citizens' needs is one of the three sets of DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams that any government can develop. Obviously, politicians' strategy diagrams and government agency business-as-usual strategy diagrams will be focused on meeting citizens' needs. However, to guarantee that citizens' needs are driving government, we need a way of clearly representing citizens' needs. When we refer to citizens here, we are implicitly including residents and people outside of the country who interact with a national government.

This Representing Citizens-Experience Maps As a DoView Strategy Diagram Explainer (B22) shows how citizen outcomes can be articulated within DoView strategy/outcomes diagrams.

Using citizen-experience maps in the form of DoView strategy diagrams can be a very efficient and powerful tool for integrating government planning across government agencies in the push for joined-up government services. You can develop citizens-experience maps (in the form of DoViews) for a whole range of different areas of citizen need, and for different sets of citizens, that a government wishes to focus on.

When used in this way, individual government agencies, providers, and initiatives can map their activities onto a common citizen-experience map to identify the touchpoints at which government activity meets citizens' needs. You do this using DoView Visual Alignment.

The Representing Citizens-Experience Maps As a DoView Strategy Diagram Explainer (B22) on the next page shows two DoView citizen-experience maps. The first is for a mainstream service for all citizens. This is for government activity focused on the second rationale from the Determining Who's Outcomes - Citizen or Government's - The Rationales for Government Assistance Framework Tool (B20). The second DoView diagram relates to citizens who need particular assistance, in this case, young people. This diagram is based on the fourth rationale within the Four Rationales Tool.
